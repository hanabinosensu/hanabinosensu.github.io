Ironowa

The latest dance title “Ironowa” debuted at Shonan Yosakoi Festival 2025, and ranked 3rd in the street performance category. It depicts amusement parks where you had fun time in your childhood.

Can you spot a naruko clapper hidden in the design of the costume? A distinctive frill and a notched collar, which are both rare in yosakoi outfit, enhance a joyful mood of the performance.

The song sounds innocent but in fact dancers are challenged with tricky rhythms like triplets, four-beat waltz, sudden meter changes, and irregular time signatures.

About

Tokyo Hanabi is a yosakoi dance team formed in 2005 by a few students from Waseda University, and now it has grown to boast 150+ members. Yosakoi is a freeform dance with two simple rules: hold wooden clappers called naruko in hands, and incorporate the yosakoibushi melody into dance songs. Hanabi uses some extra props matoi and sensu in addition to naruko as illustrated below.

Learn more: Yosakoi, Waseda University, Yosakoibushi

Amatsukasa

The 19th annual dance title “Amatsukasa” portrays an elusive dragon thriving in the sky. Guess the true identity of the dragon before proceeding to the translated lyrics below. Enjoy vigorous choreography and music!
